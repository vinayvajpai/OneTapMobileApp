There are 3 main objectives why you may want to consider running digital and social ads.

Increasing brand awareness

Brand awareness is the most common objective.

This might include ads for events, product launches, new services or even just to raise brand profile; in order to increase your business’s exposure and make it more visible on social media networks. Brand awareness is often used to develop a strong online presence and drive potential customers towards your business. This may be people who have previously not heard of you or new prospects looking for products or services in their area.

There are a few things to consider when increasing brand awareness. Firstly, it’s important that your ads are creative and interesting – people will only stop and take notice if they stand out from the rest. Secondly, you need to ensure that your target audience is being reached effectively. Branding campaigns work best when there’s a specific group of people you’re targeting, so make sure that the demographics for your ads are correct. More on that later And finally, always measure and track results to see how successful your campaign has been.

Sending traffic to your website

Another common objective is increasing website traffic. This could be through ads that drive people to your website or even just getting them to click on a link. The main aim is to increase the number of visitors who come to your site.

There are a few things you can do to achieve this:

  • Make sure your website is optimised for your ads and is easy to navigate.
  • Use effective keywords in your ads that people are likely to search for.
  • Produce interesting, engaging ad content that people will want to read and or watch.
Getting conversions

The final main objective is conversions. This could be anything from getting people to sign up for a newsletter, filling in a contact form or buying a product.

When it comes to conversion-focused ads, there are a few key things to remember:

  • Firstly, you need to identify your target audience and create ads that appeal specifically to them.
  • And secondly, make sure you’re using effective tracking methods to see how well your ads are performing and which demographics they reach best. This way you can ensure that budget is being spent in the right places and conversions will be maximised.

    • So there we have it; these three objectives represent some of the most common reasons people run digital and social ads.

When you create a digital ad campaign, it is important to make sure that you are targeting the audience who will most likely be interested in your product or service. This might seem like common sense; however, many companies make the mistake of creating their audience without considering what they want and need. We would like to offer you some tips on how to build an audience for your digital ad campaign that will help increase ROI and generate more revenue!

Start by identifying the audience you want to target. What kind of people are they? Where do they live? When do they like to shop for your product or service online and in person? Is there a specific age group, income level, or gender that is more likely than others to purchase what you're selling? If so, it's time to create your audience.

Build a profile for each audience member. Start with the easiest audience, which is those who have purchased from you before or are already fans of your brand on social media. Then move towards building an audience that might need more convincing about what you offer to make the customers as well as those who may only be browsing at the moment.

Create audience profiles that are clear and concise, but also include the information you need to target them with digital ads effectively. Then test your audience until you find one that works for your business!

"You want to budget $5,000 for your next ad." This is a sentence that many business owners dread hearing. It sounds like a lot of money and it can be hard to know where to start with budgeting. Luckily, we have some tips on how you can choose the right budget for your next ad campaign!

When budgeting for a new ad campaign, you'll need to consider your targeting. Who are you trying to reach with your ad? Once you have a good idea of your target market, it will be easier to determine how much money you need to spend on advertising. You don't want to budget too little and not reach your desired audience, but budgeting too much could lead to wasted spending.

Next, you'll need to consider what kind of ad experience your target audience is looking for. If they're interested in a video ad, it may be worth budgeting more than if you were planning on running an image-based social media campaign.

Finally, we recommend creating a budget that allows for some flexibility. Things always come up and you may need to adjust your budget as the campaign progresses. This way, you won't be too restricted in what you can do and you'll have room to make changes if needed.

By following these tips, you can create a budget that will help your ad campaign succeed!

Rule of thumb

Our rule of thumb for budgeting is:

  1. Start small. Digital and social ads are incredibly flexible. Create lots of ads with small budgets. Test out many creatives, audiences, budgets, tag lines and copy. See what works and adjust the budget and targets along the way. You can go as low as $20 and as high as you can imagine so invest wisely.
  2. The more popular the platform and the more specific your audience, the more budget you will need.
  3. Allocate more budget to things that are working. Use the 95-5 rule where you can allocate 5% of your budget to try out new and experimental strategies with your targeting, audience creation and more. If it works, scale it up and if it doesn't you can always cut it out.

You have put a lot of time and money into your ad campaign. You are now wondering how to measure the performance of your campaign. Brand awareness, traffic, and conversion rates- are all important metrics that you should be paying attention to as you start analysing the numbers from your advertising efforts.

Method # One: Brand Awareness

When measuring brand awareness, you should look at how many people you reached. Metrics like Reach, Impressions and Views are helpful (Check out the definitions section for details). You want to make sure that people are aware of your product or service and understand what it is that you offer. Brand awareness is the simplest to measure and here are a few easy ways:

  • Brand recognition - how much do people remember you?
  • Brand recall - do they know what your product/service does? Facebook and Google do the heavy lifting for you, and you can simply see how these numbers stack up.

Method # Two: Traffic

You want to make sure you are driving quality traffic to your website and that people are engaging with your content. Here are a few ways to measure traffic:

  • Unique visitors - how many different people visit your site?
  • Pageviews - how many pages do they view while on your site?
  • Bounce rate - what percentage of people leave your site after viewing only one page?

Method # Three: Conversion Rate

The goal of any ad campaign is to convert leads into customers. Measuring the conversion rate is essential in determining the success of your campaign. Here are a few ways to track conversions:

  • Goal conversion - how many people reached the goal you set? This is usually a form fill or a phone call directly from a Google Ad!
  • Lead form - what percentage of leads fill out a lead form on your website?
Rule of thumb

Our rule of thumb for measuring campaign success is:

  1. Focus on the important metrics. For example, look at Brand Awareness and you want to see how many times your ad has been served and potentially how many views your video has generated. For traffic, you want to see how many people clicked on the ad and out of those clicks how many people drove to the website? For conversions, you should measure actions like how many forms are filled or phone calls your ads generated.

Cost Per Click - CPC stands for Cost Per Click. It's a method that websites use to determine the average times an advertiser has clicked on the relevant ad.

Click-Through Rate - Generally speaking, a click-through rate is a calculated metric that measures the ratio of users who click on a specific link to the total number of users who view the page, email, advertisement, etc.

Cost Per 1000 - Cost per thousand (CPM), also called cost per mille, is a marketing term used to denote the price of 1,000 advertisement impressions on one web page. If a website publisher charges $2.00 CPM, that means an advertiser must pay $2.00 for every 1,000 impressions of its ad.

Impressions - Impressions are the number of times your content is displayed, no matter if it was clicked or not. Think of reach as the number of unique people who see your content. In a perfect world, every one of your followers would see every piece of content you posted.

Video Views - How many times a video has been watched?

15-second view - How many times a video has been watched for at least 15 seconds?

Cost Per Call - How much are you spending to get a single call as a result of utilising ads?

Conversion Rate - Conversion rate is defined as the number of visitors to a website that completes the desired goal (a conversion) out of the total number of visitors.

Frequency - the average number of times that each person saw your ad.
